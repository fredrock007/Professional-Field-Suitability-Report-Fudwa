4. Psychological & Pedagogical Evidence

Cognitive psychology research shows that learners remember content better when teachers activate prior knowledge, use multiple representations, and emphasise meaning rather than memorisation. Fudwa Thomas consistently does the following:

  • Links new ideas to familiar places (Durban, Gauteng gold mines, sugarcane regions).
  • Pairs definitions with practical consequences (negative GDP leading to job losses and currency decline).
  • Uses visual supports: graphs, maps, infographics, and text.
  • Asks metacognitive questions such as “What do you think they are asking when they say ‘range’?”
  • Builds learner confidence by highlighting marks achieved and progress made.

These patterns indicate well-developed pedagogical content knowledge: not just knowing the Geography or Economics, but knowing how to make that knowledge learnable.
